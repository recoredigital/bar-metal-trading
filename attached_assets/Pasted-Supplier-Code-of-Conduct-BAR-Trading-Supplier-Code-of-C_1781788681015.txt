Supplier Code of Conduct
BAR Trading – Supplier Code of Conduct
Last Updated: June 2026
BAR Trading is committed to conducting business ethically, responsibly and in accordance with applicable laws and regulations. We expect all suppliers, subcontractors, service providers and business partners to uphold the principles outlined in this Supplier Code of Conduct.
1. Compliance with Laws
Suppliers must comply with all applicable laws, regulations and industry requirements in the countries in which they operate.
This includes, but is not limited to:
•	Employment legislation
•	Health and safety regulations
•	Environmental regulations
•	Trade and export controls
•	Anti-bribery and anti-corruption legislation
2. Ethical Business Conduct
Suppliers must conduct business honestly and ethically at all times.
Suppliers shall not:
•	Offer or accept bribes, kickbacks or improper payments
•	Engage in fraud, deception or misrepresentation
•	Falsify records, certifications or documentation
•	Participate in any unlawful or unethical business practices
3. Human Rights and Labour Standards
BAR Trading expects suppliers to respect internationally recognised human rights.
Suppliers must:
•	Prohibit forced, bonded or involuntary labour
•	Prohibit child labour
•	Provide fair wages and working conditions
•	Respect freedom of association where permitted by law
•	Maintain a workplace free from discrimination and harassment
4. Health and Safety
Suppliers are expected to provide a safe and healthy working environment for employees, contractors and visitors.
Appropriate measures should be implemented to:
•	Identify workplace hazards
•	Reduce health and safety risks
•	Provide suitable training
•	Comply with relevant health and safety legislation
5. Environmental Responsibility
BAR Trading supports responsible environmental practices throughout the supply chain.
Suppliers are encouraged to:
•	Minimise waste generation
•	Reduce energy consumption where practical
•	Manage materials responsibly
•	Comply with environmental legislation and permits
•	Promote recycling and resource efficiency
6. Material Integrity and Traceability
Where applicable, suppliers should maintain accurate records regarding:
•	Material origin
•	Material specifications
•	Certifications
•	Testing data
•	Chain of custody information
All documentation supplied to BAR Trading should be accurate and complete.
7. Responsible Sourcing
Suppliers should take reasonable steps to ensure that materials supplied have been sourced responsibly and legally.
BAR Trading reserves the right to request supporting documentation where appropriate.
8. Confidentiality
Suppliers must protect confidential business information obtained through their relationship with BAR Trading and must not disclose such information without appropriate authorisation.
9. Reporting Concerns
Suppliers are encouraged to raise concerns regarding suspected unethical, illegal or unsafe practices relating to business activities involving BAR Trading.
10. Compliance with this Code
BAR Trading expects suppliers to operate in accordance with the principles set out in this Code.
Failure to comply may affect future business relationships.
